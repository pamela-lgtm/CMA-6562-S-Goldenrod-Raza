Based on current market data for comparable 2BR/2BA units in Carter Glen and the immediate South Goldenrod corridor, we recommend listing at $1,950 per month — the top of the active and leased comp range. The Cost of Getting Pricing Wrong
Overpricing is the single most expensive mistake a landlord can make
One of the comparable properties in this analysis — 6568 S. Goldenrod Rd — sat on the market for 132 days before leasing. That is not a market problem. That is a pricing problem. The property was listed too high and the price was not reduced quickly enough. The owner paid for that decision with 4+ months of vacancy. At $1,900/month, that represents over $8,400 in lost income — income that can never be recovered, regardless of what rent is eventually achieved.
The Verandah approach
We price accurately from day one and we move decisively if the market signals we need to adjust. We do not let pride of price cost our owners money. Every other leased comp in this analysis leased at 100% of asking price with days on market ranging from 24 to 77 days. Accurate pricing works. Holding too long does not.

What the Active Listing tells us — and doesn't
There is one active listing currently on the market at $2,000 (6448 S. Goldenrod Rd, 23 days on market). While it is useful as a reference point, active listings reflect what owners want to receive — not what the market is actually paying. The leased comps are what matter. They show what qualified tenants are actually signing leases for. Every leased comp in this analysis closed at exactly 100% of asking price, with lease prices ranging from $1,900 to $2,000. The data supports $1,950 as a strong, justified starting position.
